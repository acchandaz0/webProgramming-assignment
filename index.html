About Cats

The cat (Felis catus) is a small domesticated carnivorous mammal with soft fur, a short snout, and retractable claws. It is widely kept as a pet or for catching mice, and many breeds have been developed.
Conservation status: Domesticated
Common name: Cat
Scientific name: Felis catus
Type: Mammals
Diet: Carnivore
Size: 28 inches
Weight: 5 to 20 pounds

Behind A Cat’s Cuteness

What the Science Says. Like babies, cats seem to activate our brain's cuteness response. This might be due to cats' physical characteristics, including child-like features like big eyes and small noses. Studies have shown that humans, particularly women, extend the “baby schema effect” to animals as well as babies. here are 10 other reasons cats are so cute:
  1. Their squishy paws are pawsitively irresistible.
  2. They knead the sweetest biscuits.
  3. They have the softest, silliest bellies.
  4. They take napping to the (adorable) extreme.
  5. Their tails have minds of their own.
  6. They’re not shy about wanting pets.
  7. They have the sweetest meows.
  8. They bring us “gifts.”
  9. They know when we’re sad.
  10. Even their butts are cute.

Do they think you’re cute?

Although cats can't give us the answer to this question, studies have led researchers to believe cats do not think people are "cute." Cats appear to treat humans like they treat other cats — so they may think we are larger, hairless cats. They don't seem to be aware we are a separate species and only see us as their "owners" or parents because we feed and care for them.
